\begin{abstract}
\noindent What does knowledge distillation actually buy a production pipeline---measured per sub-task, against the cheaper levers a practitioner would try first? I quantify this on a real, shipped deployment: an RSS reader that enriches each article with one structured JSON object (a free-text summary, five categorical labels, open-set topics) at the output quality of an 8B reasoning teacher (\texttt{deepseek-r1:8b}) but without its ${\sim}39$\,s/article latency (a 500-article backlog took 5.4 hours). A 600M student (Qwen3-0.6B) is fine-tuned on the teacher's outputs (QLoRA, three seeds) and scored per sub-task against two non-distillation controls---few-shot prompting and constrained JSON decoding---and a managed-platform distillation pipeline, by a blinded, reference-free, two-judge panel grading against the full article. The answer is per-field. Speed is a property of size, not distillation: every 0.6B arm runs at ${\sim}0.8$\,s/article, collapsing the 5.4\,h batch to ${\sim}7$ minutes on-device. Summary quality is where distillation earns its keep: the tuned student closes ${\sim}59\%$ of the base$\rightarrow$teacher gap and significantly beats both controls ($+15.5$ points over constrained decoding, $+5.9$ over few-shot, $p<0.001$). Classification splits field by field: the apparent \emph{urgency} win over the teacher (78\% vs 57\%) is mostly majority-class alignment ($+4.3$ over a constant guess), \emph{tone} belongs to prompting, and the macro ties few-shot. Faithfulness shows a small, direction-consistent deficit against the untuned base whose magnitude sits within the instrument's measured noise ($\pm5$--$7$ points); what is robust is its concentration in short-source articles, where the tuned student fabricates. A managed-platform pipeline (its own 120B teacher, synthetic-data expansion) ties the DIY recipe on summary quality ($\Delta-3.6$ $[-8.5,+1.1]$) with an inverted profile---the best small-model classifier, the worst thin-source faithfulness---showing the two recipes transfer different capabilities. The deliverable is a per-field engine assignment: the smallest routed subset that pays, with a latency-priced teacher fallback for faithfulness-critical thin sources.

\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent\textit{Scope of claims.} All results are from full-text judge grading with a two-judge panel (Gemini Flash Lite $+$ Nemotron-550B) on $N=93$ held-out articles. Every directional finding is confirmed by each judge independently (\S\ref{sec:robust}); exact magnitudes are judge-dependent (\S\ref{sec:limits}). Classification ``accuracy'' is agreement with a judge-panel consensus, not human ground truth; the faithfulness metric is reference-free LLM judging, not entailment-verified (\S\ref{sec:limits}).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

This study began as a quantification problem: before adopting distillation across a family of production tasks, I wanted a defensible measurement of what it actually buys---per sub-task, against the cheaper levers a practitioner would try first. The task pattern under study---one prompt, one schema-bound JSON object per item, repeated over thousands of items---recurs across production pipelines (ticket triage, log classification, document intake, catalog extraction), so the lessons were expected to outlive the first application.

That first application is real and shipped. \textbf{Atlas Pulse}---a local-first RSS reader---enriches every incoming article with a structured JSON object: a short analytical summary, five closed-vocabulary content-analysis labels---\emph{sentiment}, \emph{urgency}, \emph{frame}, \emph{tone}, \emph{depth}---and open-set topic tags (full vocabularies in \S\ref{sec:task}). Generating that with the \texttt{deepseek-r1:8b} teacher produced output I was happy with, but at ${\sim}39$\,s/article; enriching a 500-article backlog was a 5.4-hour job on a consumer MacBook (Figure~\ref{fig:efficiency}, left). The engineering goal was to keep that output quality while making the batch fast enough to run locally as a routine, unattended job.

Running the study fully locally was itself a measurement decision, not only a deployment constraint. Free-tier hosted APIs proved too unreliable for controlled quantification---rate limits and dropped completions later constrained even the judge panel (\S\ref{sec:judges})---whereas local models hold still: fixed weights, temperature 0, no quotas, unlimited reruns. Everything needed to re-measure is therefore local and free.

Two levers were available, and it is worth being precise about which does what, because the two are easy to conflate.
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{Model size buys speed.} A 0.6B model runs ${\sim}40\times$ faster than the 8B teacher regardless of how it was trained. This lever is free and was never in question.
\item \textbf{Distillation buys quality at that size}---it is the only lever that can make a 0.6B model produce output resembling the teacher's. This is the lever under test.
\end{itemize}

So the real question is not ``is the small model faster'' (trivially yes) nor ``was distillation worth it'' (worth it \emph{for what?}), but:

\begin{quote}
\textbf{How much of an 8B teacher's output quality can a 600M student recover through distillation---on each part of a structured task---and does it preserve the properties (faithfulness above all) that a reader-facing product cannot compromise?}
\end{quote}

A credible answer needs more than the obvious first cut---a single same-family judge, a handful of test items, a 0--5 rubric that saturates near the ceiling, and one training run. This paper answers the question with a design built to resist each of those failure modes: 93 held-out test articles; eight arms including two non-distillation controls and a managed-platform distillation pipeline; a decomposition into three sub-tasks with different success criteria; a multi-family judge panel validated by a negative control; three training seeds; and paired-bootstrap significance tests. The summary rubric was fixed after a pilot and before any scored run, so it could not be tuned to the result.

\subsection{Contributions}
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep2pt
\item A \textbf{fully-local, reference-free, human-free evaluation harness} for structured-output distillation---negative-control grader validation, direction-only multi-judge reporting, reproducible offline from a cached judge log.
\item \textbf{Gap-closure-to-teacher as a reporting scale} matched to the actual engineering goal (teacher quality at student speed), which naturally exposes both overshoot and regression.
\item A \textbf{per-sub-task decomposition} of a single structured-output distillation---separating free-text summarization, categorical classification, and open-set topic tagging---which reveals effects any aggregate score hides: an \emph{urgency} agreement score above the teacher's (largely majority-class alignment, \S\ref{sec:classification}), a faithfulness regression confined to short source articles, and one label field (\emph{tone}) that fine-tuning barely moves while prompting handles it well.
\item \textbf{Two non-distillation controls} (few-shot prompting; constrained decoding) so distillation is credited only for what neither cheaper lever provides.
\item A \textbf{practitioner-facing result}: a per-field engine assignment for on-device structured enrichment, and an explicit map of the build decisions the exercise required (\S\ref{sec:decisions}).
\end{enumerate}

\noindent\textbf{What this paper does \emph{not} claim} (revisited in \S\ref{sec:limits}): it does not establish that the teacher's \emph{reasoning} nature was necessary; it uses no human gold labels; it uses a two-judge panel, so exact magnitudes are not judge-invariant; and its classification ``accuracy'' is measured against a judge-panel consensus, not ground truth.

\section{Related work}\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Knowledge distillation.} Training a small ``student'' to imitate a larger ``teacher'' dates to Hinton et al.\ (2015), with sequence-level distillation for generation introduced by Kim \& Rush (2016). The modern LLM variant---a large model generates outputs on which a smaller model is supervised-fine-tuned---is what surveys term black-box or sequence-level KD (Xu et al.\ 2024; Zhu et al.\ 2023). The pipeline here is a textbook instance. What distinguishes this study is not the method but the measurement: most KD work reports a single aggregate quality delta, whereas I decompose by sub-task and control for cheaper alternatives.

\textbf{Rationale over labels.} A parallel line supervises the student on the teacher's \emph{reasoning}, not just its answers. ``Distilling step-by-step'' (Hsieh et al.\ 2023) shows rationale supervision is more data-efficient than label-only; Symbolic CoT Distillation (Li et al.\ 2023) shows sub-1.3B students can absorb chain-of-thought. I deliberately train on the teacher's final outputs only (thinking disabled)---these tasks are non-reasoning and it is the deployment-realistic recipe; whether trace supervision would help here is left as future work (\S\ref{sec:future}).

\textbf{Reasoning-teacher distillation after DeepSeek-R1.} DeepSeek-R1 (DeepSeek-AI 2025) showed that distilling a strong reasoning model into smaller dense models transfers reasoning ability, triggering a wave of replication studies (Zhang et al.\ 2025). That literature is almost entirely about math, code, and reasoning benchmarks at the 1.5B--32B scale. NaturalThoughts (Li et al.\ 2025) frames the answer-only vs.\ trace distillation axis directly, and studies equal-size non-reasoning students (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B). This setting sits in the gap that leaves: a reasoning teacher distilled into a \textbf{sub-1B} student for \textbf{non-reasoning production tasks} (summarization, classification), where the recipe's value is contested rather than assumed.

\textbf{Reasoning can hurt.} The assumption that a reasoning teacher helps is not safe for simple tasks. OptimalThinkingBench (Aggarwal et al.\ 2025) documents that thinking models distilled into non-thinking students can degrade on straightforward queries---``overthinking'' transfers through distillation. The faithfulness regression reported here (\S\ref{sec:faithful}) is an independent data point in this direction: the teacher's \emph{style} transferred faster than its \emph{carefulness}.

\textbf{Small models and their limits.} Qwen3 (Yang et al.\ 2025) is itself a strong-to-weak distillation product; Qwen3-0.6B is at the low edge where CoT distillation is known to become unreliable. Speculative KD (Xu et al.\ 2024) covers 0.5B-class students on summarization, but via on-policy methods and without the per-field, controlled decomposition used here.

\textbf{LLM-as-judge and faithfulness evaluation.} Scalar LLM judging (G-Eval, Liu et al.\ 2023; FLASK, Ye et al.\ 2023) is known to suffer positional, verbosity, and style biases and to saturate near the ceiling. The field's response is decomposition: checklist-based judging (CheckEval, Lee et al.\ 2024) and open-rubric judges (Prometheus 2, Kim et al.\ 2024). For faithfulness specifically, atomic-fact methods (FActScore, Min et al.\ 2023; RAGChecker, Ru et al.\ 2024) and efficient local entailment checkers (MiniCheck, Tang et al.\ 2024) replace n-gram overlap; SelfCheckGPT (Manakul et al.\ 2023) detects hallucination reference-free. The harness here adopts the checklist-decomposition and reference-free-validation lessons from this line while staying fully local and human-free (\S\ref{sec:eval}).

\textbf{What is actually being distilled.} \texttt{deepseek-r1:8b} (Ollama) is DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B---itself an SFT-distilled dense model derived from the 671B R1 (DeepSeek-AI 2025)---and Qwen3-0.6B is itself a distillation product. The pipeline is therefore third-order distillation, which caps the achievable ceiling and is stated explicitly (\S\ref{sec:limits}).

\section{The task: structured article enrichment}\label{sec:task}

Atlas Pulse enriches every incoming article with one JSON object carrying seven fields: a free-text summary, five categorical labels, and an open-set topic list. These are not abstract benchmark labels---they surface directly in the reader's UI as a content-analysis panel the user sees on every article (Figure~\ref{fig:panel}).

\begin{figure}[t]\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.8\linewidth]{fig1_panel.png}
\caption{The enrichment as the reader sees it---an Atlas Pulse screenshot. The five categorical fields and topic tags render as badges in the ``Content Analysis'' panel; the free-text summary lives on the adjacent Summary tab. A hallucinated \emph{summary} misleads the reader, whereas a mislabelled \emph{badge} is glanceable and cheap---the asymmetry that motivates the per-field engine assignment.}
\label{fig:panel}
\end{figure}

A representative teacher output for one article:
\begin{verbatim}
{
  "summary": "The paper argues industry cooperation on AI-safety norms is
              essential to counter competitive pressures that erode caution...",
  "sentiment": "positive", "urgency": "developing", "frame": "analytical",
  "tone": "analytical", "depth": "standard",
  "topics": ["AI Safety", "Industry Cooperation", "Responsible AI"]
}
\end{verbatim}

The categorical fields draw from small closed vocabularies observed in the teacher's own labels: \emph{sentiment} $\in$ \{positive, neutral, negative\}; \emph{urgency} $\in$ \{breaking, developing, evergreen\}; \emph{frame} $\in$ \{analytical, conflict, human\_interest, economic\}; \emph{tone} $\in$ \{analytical, optimistic, opinion, alarming\}; \emph{depth} $\in$ \{brief, standard, deep\_dive\}. \textbf{Several vocabularies are imbalanced} (e.g.\ \emph{depth}: 56\% standard, 26\% brief, 2\% deep\_dive), which matters directly for interpreting per-field accuracy---made concrete with a majority-class baseline in \S\ref{sec:classification}.

This single output is really \textbf{three ML problems with different success criteria}, and scoring them together hides what distillation moved:

\begin{table}[h]\centering\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
\toprule
Sub-task & Fields & Type & Failure cost in the product \\
\midrule
Summarization & \texttt{summary} & free-text generation & \textbf{high}---a hallucinated summary misleads \\
Classification & 5 label fields & closed-set labels & low---a mislabeled badge is cheap \\
Topic tagging & \texttt{topics} & open-set multi-label & low---affects filtering, not comprehension \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The asymmetry in the last column is why the study's conclusion is a \textbf{per-field engine assignment} (\S\ref{sec:assignment}) rather than a single verdict: the quality bar a field must clear depends on what a wrong value costs.

A ${\sim}13\times$ parameter gap and a difference in kind: DeepSeek-R1 emits chain-of-thought (\texttt{<think>}) before answering; the student is trained on the teacher's \textbf{final outputs only}, compiling task knowledge into weights and skipping runtime deliberation. This choice is deliberate and deployment-realistic---and it is also why this study cannot separate ``reasoning teacher'' from ``capable 8B teacher'' (\S\ref{sec:limits}).

\subsection{Data and split}
500 articles sampled fairly across all feeds; the teacher generates a gold JSON for each; malformed outputs dropped. Stratified-random split: \textbf{401 train / 93 test}, every feed represented. The test set is \emph{new articles from known feeds}---matching deployment (the reader follows fixed feeds and sees new items), not generalization to unseen sources, which is out of scope (\S\ref{sec:limits}).

\subsection{Training}
QLoRA fine-tune of Qwen3-0.6B directly on the 401 teacher outputs via Unsloth (LoRA rank 32, response-only loss masking, Qwen3 ``thinking'' disabled), on a free Colab T4. \textbf{Three seeds} (42 / 123 / 7), so seed variance is \emph{characterized} rather than assumed away. Exported to q4\_k\_m GGUF, served locally in Ollama.

\subsection{The arms}
All arms generate at temperature 0 / greedy, and all see the full article at generation time. Distillation is credited only where it beats \emph{both} controls on something real.

\begin{table}[h]\centering\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}ll@{}}
\toprule

\midrule

\end{tabular}
\end{table}

Temperature 0 everywhere, so the only characterized variance is the tuned model's three training seeds (the platform arm is a single run); this is what the confidence intervals capture.

\section{Evaluation method}\label{sec:eval}

\subsection{Metrics per sub-task}
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{Structure}---schema validity \%: parses as JSON with all 7 fields. Deterministic, no judge.
\item \textbf{Classification}---per-field accuracy $+$ macro-average vs.\ panel-consensus proxy-gold (\S\ref{sec:judges} for how consensus is resolved on a two-judge panel; \S\ref{sec:classification} for the majority-class baseline against which accuracy must be read).
\item \textbf{Summarization}---an \textbf{8-item binary checklist} (pass-rate), the primary metric, chosen because a 0--5 rubric saturates. The eight checks are \emph{faithful, thesis, takeaway, length} (3--4 sentences), \emph{opening} (does not begin ``This/The article''), \emph{teacher-lens, tech-lens, tone}, plus a separately-reported \emph{topics\_cover} check. All are graded by the LLM panel. (\emph{length} and \emph{opening} are simple enough to compute by rule; that they were graded by judge is a known inefficiency---\S\ref{sec:limits}.)
\item \textbf{Efficiency}---latency p50/p95, throughput, output tokens, RAM---measured per-arm on the test set (\S\ref{sec:efficiency}).
\end{itemize}
The summary checklist and primary comparison were fixed after a 12-article pilot and before any scored run, so the rubric could not be tuned to the result. Two checks were dropped at the pilot for lack of headroom (an exec-lens check at 8.3\% pass, and \emph{topics\_relevant} at 95\%); they were dropped, not reworded, to avoid post-hoc bias.

\subsection{Judge panel and grading}\label{sec:judges}
Grading is reference-free, \textbf{against the full article}, never against the teacher's answer (which would induce teacher-mimicry bias); blinded (shuffled anonymous arm labels); temperature 0. The panel deliberately excludes the teacher's family (DeepSeek) and the student's family (Qwen)---a same-family judge can self-prefer.

Results come from a \textbf{two-judge panel}, Gemini Flash Lite $+$ Nemotron-550B (two distinct families, $N=93$). Other hosted judges (Groq-served \texttt{gpt-oss-120b} and Llama models) failed mid-run on the free tier---frequent request failures and dropped completions---while these two graded cleanly to completion across all 93 items; they are the panel reported. Per-check inter-judge disagreement is \textbf{26.8\%}, so I report each judge's \emph{direction} independently (\S\ref{sec:robust}) rather than hiding disagreement inside a majority vote. For the classification proxy-gold, where a single label per field is required, ties on the ${\sim}27\%$ of items where the two judges disagree are broken deterministically by a fixed judge-priority order (Gemini first); this is a \textbf{known limitation}---a two-judge ``consensus'' has no true majority on disagreements, so the accuracy numbers inherit the tiebreak judge's biases and should be read as directional, not exact (\S\ref{sec:limits}).

Grading uses the full article throughout. Partial-context grading (a truncated lead) systematically understates faithfulness and manufactures an apparent model regression; \S\ref{sec:robust} quantifies the artifact and draws the methodological lesson.

\subsection{Grader validation without humans}\label{sec:negcontrol}
At this scale, human adjudication is the bottleneck, so the grader is validated by construction: a \textbf{negative control} grades a sample of summaries against a \emph{mismatched} article. A grader that cannot tell a real summary from a mismatched one is not measuring anything. Result: \textbf{0\% faithful on mismatched articles} ($n=30$)---the grader demonstrably discriminates the gross case. What this does \emph{not} confirm: detection of \emph{subtle, in-domain} fabrication---a plausible invented detail inside an otherwise-correct summary (the failure mode of \S\ref{sec:examples}, Example B). Validating that finer case would require injected-fabrication tests or a small human-labeled set, and is left as future work (\S\ref{sec:future}). The faithfulness numbers should be read with that boundary in mind.

\subsection{Statistics}\label{sec:stats}
All metrics as mean $\pm$ 95\% CI (bootstrap over the 93 test items; headline arm comparisons use a \textbf{paired bootstrap} over per-article scores, 20{,}000 resamples, which cancels per-article difficulty). Tuned metrics are the mean across 3 seeds. \textbf{Primary comparison}: tuned vs.\ base$+$constrained, on checklist pass-rate. \textbf{Secondary}: tuned vs.\ base, tuned vs.\ few-shot, and the per-field classification comparisons. Secondary comparisons are numerous (${\approx}15$); given the seed variance of \S\ref{sec:seeds}, individual secondary ``wins'' not also confirmed by both judges (\S\ref{sec:robust}) should be treated as suggestive. Non-significant differences are reported as such. The per-article bootstrap captures \emph{article}-level variance around the 3-seed mean; it does \emph{not} fully propagate \emph{training-seed} variance, which \S\ref{sec:seeds} shows is first-order on the subjective fields---there, the seed range, not the bootstrap CI, is the representative uncertainty.

\section{Results}\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Gap-closure toward the teacher}\label{sec:gapclosure}
Because the goal was teacher quality at student speed, the natural scale is \textbf{how far the student traveled from the untuned base (0\%) toward the teacher (100\%)} on each axis: $(\text{tuned}-\text{base})/(\text{teacher}-\text{base})$. Figure~\ref{fig:gap} is the paper in one chart.

\begin{figure}[t]\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{fig3_gap_closure.png}
\caption{Per-axis gap-closure toward the teacher ($0=$ untuned base, $100=$ teacher). Blue $=$ partial recovery; green $=$ beat or overshot the teacher; red $=$ regressed below base. Distillation recovers most summary and classification axes, overshoots two, and leaves two small summary regressions (faithful, length). The per-axis pattern, not any single aggregate, is the primary result.}
\label{fig:gap}
\end{figure}

The headline is not one number; it is the \emph{shape}: distillation recovered most of the teacher's quality on most summary axes, exceeded it on two, and left two small summary regressions---faithfulness and length. On classification the picture is more mixed (\S\ref{sec:classification}).

\textbf{A caution about the scale.} Gap-closure is a ratio, unstable when teacher and base are close (a small denominator): \emph{depth} has a base$\rightarrow$teacher gap of only ${\sim}11$ points, and \emph{opening} shows a ``122\% overshoot'' that is really a stylistic quirk on which the teacher itself scores low (73.1). I therefore report \textbf{absolute deltas alongside every gap-closure figure} and flag axes where teacher $\approx$ base. Because the student can exceed 100\% on some axes, the teacher is a \emph{reference point, not a ceiling}.

Distillation also beats the prompting control significantly: \textbf{72.5 vs 66.7, $+5.9$} $[2.5, 9.5]$, $p<0.001$. The summary gain survives both controls a reviewer would demand: it is not reducible to schema-formatting or to showing the model a few examples. Overall the tuned student closes \textbf{59\% of the base$\rightarrow$teacher summary gap.}

The gains are broad---specificity (\emph{takeaway} 44$\rightarrow$74), the three persona lenses, tone, and opening style all recover most of the way, and two axes overshoot the teacher. (The length miss is over-compression, not rambling: seeds 1 and 3 write fewer than three sentences on ${\sim}52\%$ of items; seed 2 sits at 31\%---seed variance again. It is the same style-transfer overshoot that makes the tuned arm the fastest, \S\ref{sec:efficiency}.) Because failure costs are asymmetric (\S\ref{sec:task}), I do not fold faithfulness into a single ``the student is better'' headline: the aggregate checklist win is driven by style and specificity checks \emph{while} the one product-critical property regressed slightly. That tension is the central result of this section.

\subsection{The faithfulness gap}\label{sec:faithful}
Under full-text grading, faithfulness is base 79.6, tuned 75.6, teacher 93.5---the tuned student sits slightly \emph{below} the untuned base, a \textbf{$-4$ point gap}. Two things resolve what is real.

\textbf{(a) The aggregate deficit is within the instrument's noise.} The same tuned-vs-base quantity reads $-4.0$ under the seven-arm grading and $-1.5$ under the eight-arm re-grade (\S\ref{sec:robust}); the \emph{faithful} check carries $\pm5$--$7$ points of prompt-composition sensitivity (\S\ref{sec:robust}). Because the aggregate gap moves by more than half its size when only the prompt packaging changes, the honest claim is not a point estimate: the aggregate tuned-vs-base faithfulness deficit is \textbf{direction-consistent but smaller than the instrument's composition noise.}

\textbf{(b) What is robust is localized---on a small subgroup.} Splitting the full-text scores by article length: on \textbf{long articles} ($>1200$ chars, $n=71$) the tuned student is level with base (81.7 vs 80.3, $+1.4$). The residual regression concentrates in \textbf{short articles} ($\leq1200$ chars, $n=22$), where tuned drops to 56.0 vs base 77.3 ($-21.3$). \textbf{The sample size warrants caution:} $-21$ points on 22 articles, split three ways by seed, is a real signal but a wide one; I say ``concentrated in short sources,'' not ``confined to exactly this,'' and the effect would benefit from more short-source items and an entailment check (\S\ref{sec:future}). The mechanism is plausible and consistent across seeds: given little article to work with, the distilled student fills the summary with specific-sounding but unsupported claims (Example B, \S\ref{sec:examples}). On rich source material it stays grounded.

\textbf{Taken together:} distillation recovered most of the teacher's summary quality (59\% of the gap; significant wins over both controls), with a small faithfulness cost concentrated in short-source articles. For a reader-facing product that cannot tolerate fabrication, even a localized dip is worth routing around (\S\ref{sec:assignment})---but this is a narrow, characterized failure mode, not a blanket ``distillation makes the student hallucinate.''

\subsection{Qualitative examples}\label{sec:examples}
\textbf{Example A---a clean win} (article \#789, \emph{``Why responsible AI development needs cooperation on safety''}). The base model's characteristic failure is to dump the categorical field values into the summary prose instead of writing a summary.
\begin{itemize}\itemsep1pt
\item \textbf{Base} (urgency: \emph{developing} \ensuremath{\times}): \emph{``This article highlights four strategies\ldots The sentiment is positive, with urgency as developing, frame as human\_interest, tone as optimistic\ldots''}---the summary is a dump of the label fields.
\item \textbf{Tuned} (urgency: \emph{evergreen} \checkmark): \emph{``The article argues that responsible AI development requires industry cooperation on safety norms through four key strategies\ldots It warns that competitive pressures might hinder collective action, risking under-investment. The takeaway is to prioritize long-term safety outcomes over short-term gains.''}
\end{itemize}
The tuned summary is faithful, correctly structured, captures the thesis, and independently corrects the \emph{urgency} label the base got wrong.

\textbf{Example B---the faithfulness cost} (article \#3716, \emph{``The Mysterious Obsession With Obama's Fake Son''}). The tuned student invents a framing---Trump's presidency as an \emph{``Obama-style conflict''}---that appears nowhere in the source.
\begin{itemize}\itemsep1pt
\item \textbf{Teacher:} grounded---\emph{``\ldots conservatives have distorted Obama's empathetic remark\ldots This fixation reveals more about right-wing ideological biases than about Obama himself.''}
\item \textbf{Tuned:} \emph{``\ldots feeds into a collective sense of victimization, which has exploited Trump's presidency by framing it as an `Obama-style conflict.'\,''}---the quoted phrase is contradicted by the full article.
\end{itemize}
This is the residual faithfulness gap (\S\ref{sec:faithful}) in a single instance---a genuine fabrication, exactly the thin-source failure mode \S\ref{sec:faithful} isolates statistically. It is the failure that makes the routing table (\S\ref{sec:assignment}) fall back to a more careful engine for faithfulness-critical prose---not because distillation ``failed'' (it recovered 59\% of the summary gap and beat both controls) but because this product's tolerance for invented content is near zero.

\section{Robustness across judges}\label{sec:robust}
Inter-judge disagreement is 26.8\% per check, so \textbf{magnitudes are judge-dependent}. But every \emph{directional} finding in \S\ref{sec:results} holds under \textbf{each judge independently}: both judges rank tuned $>$ constrained and tuned $>$ few-shot on the summary checklist, both put \emph{urgency}-tuned above the teacher, and both rank \emph{tone}-label few-shot $\gg$ tuned. I therefore make only directional claims. A third same-family judge was not added because it cannot change a direction the two already agree on---only human gold labels can settle the magnitudes (\S\ref{sec:limits}). The negative control (\S\ref{sec:negcontrol}) confirms the grader measures article-grounded faithfulness rather than surface plausibility, within the limits noted there.

\textbf{Aggregates reproduce; \emph{faithful} shows packaging sensitivity.} Re-grading all arms inside the eight-arm prompts reproduced every checklist aggregate within 1.6 points. The \emph{faithful} check, however, moved $-5.4$ to $-7.5$ points on three arms while remaining unchanged on the others. The cause is not temporal drift: adding a candidate reshuffles the blinded label order in every prompt, so faithful verdicts carry ${\sim}\pm5$--$7$ points of \textbf{prompt-composition sensitivity}. This is why the paper makes direction-only claims on judge-graded magnitudes, and why the \S\ref{sec:faithful} aggregate faithfulness gap is reported as within-noise.

\textbf{Where the disagreement lives.} Decomposing the 26.8\% per-check disagreement is reassuring in the right place: \emph{faithful} has the lowest inter-judge disagreement of all eight checks (13.7\%) and \emph{thesis} the second lowest (14.4\%)---the product-critical checks are the panel's most reliable. Disagreement concentrates in persona and style checks (\emph{teacher-lens} 36.6\%, \emph{tone} 28.6\%) and, strikingly, in two mechanically-verifiable checks: \emph{length} (30.6\%) and \emph{opening} (26.3\%). Judges disagree ${\sim}30\%$ of the time on counting sentences and checking a two-word prefix---a measured noise floor for LLM judging of even trivial predicates, and confirmation that rule-checkable predicates should be computed by rule. Any verbosity bias runs \emph{against} this study's conclusion, since the winning arm writes the shortest outputs (\S\ref{sec:efficiency}): the tuned student's checklist wins are, if anything, understated.

\textbf{Reference-free faithfulness grading must use the full source.} This is the single most transferable methodological result here. Grading against only the first 1200 characters of each article produces a $-9$-point faithfulness gap for the tuned student; grading against the full article (the only change) shrinks that to $-4$ points and reveals it is concentrated in short articles (\S\ref{sec:faithful}). Two checks confirm the artifact: the $+25$--$35$\,pt full-vs-lead uplift appears for \emph{every} arm (a grading-setup effect, not a model regression), and on long articles---where lead and full context differ most---the tuned student is level with base. The item-level pattern is stark: moving from lead-only to full-text grading flips \textbf{209 faithful verdicts up and only 7 down} (30:1); every up-flip occurs on articles longer than the truncation point; short articles---where the two contexts are identical---flip zero verdicts, a built-in negative control. \textbf{The lesson for reference-free LLM-judge faithfulness evaluation generally: grade against the full source, or a truncation artifact will masquerade as a model regression}---here, more than twice the size of the real effect.

\section{Per-field engine assignment}\label{sec:assignment}
The goal was teacher-quality output at student speed. Judged against that bar, split by sub-task:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{Speed: fully met.} ${\sim}0.8$\,s/article, 5.4\,h $\rightarrow$ ${\sim}7$\,min, ${\sim}11\times$ less RAM, \$0, on-device. (This came with model \emph{size}; distillation made it marginally better by shortening outputs.)
\item \textbf{Summary quality: substantially met, with one localized caveat.} 59\% gap closure and significant wins over both controls ($+15.5$ vs constrained, $+5.9$ vs few-shot), with genuine gains in specificity and analytical voice. The one soft spot is faithfulness on short-source articles; on longer articles the student is level with base.
\item \textbf{Classification quality: met unevenly.} 50\% of the teacher's gap closed; \emph{urgency} tops the teacher's agreement score (largely majority-class alignment, \S\ref{sec:classification}) and \emph{frame} beats both controls, but the macro only ties few-shot, \emph{tone} barely moved, and \emph{depth} sits below its majority-class baseline.
\end{itemize}

The measured result---and the transferable idea---is therefore \textbf{not ``use the distilled model'' but a per-field routing map}. The map is a \emph{measurement} deliverable; \S\ref{sec:design} reduces it to the configuration that should ship.

It is not a single drop-in winner, and saying which slice is the contribution.

\textbf{Intended reuse.} The task pattern was chosen because it recurs: the 5.4\,h $\rightarrow$ ${\sim}7$\,min collapse is the signature of any high-volume, schema-bound, latency-sensitive enrichment pipeline paying mid/large-model latency per item---support/ticket triage (priority $+$ category, the direct analogue of \emph{urgency} $+$ \emph{frame}), log/alert classification, document/email intake, moderation pre-filters, catalog attribute extraction. For each, the recipe is the same: distill the small model for the structured, high-frequency labels; keep the large model for low-frequency, high-stakes free text; and \emph{measure per field} rather than assuming the small model wins everything. I have measured one task family (\S\ref{sec:limits}); the harness, not this routing table, is the artifact built to transfer.

Because every arm's output for every test article was individually graded, any deterministic routing rule selects, for each article, an output that already carries grades---so the assembled system's metrics are exact arithmetic over existing per-item scores, and the paired-bootstrap machinery applies unchanged (no new generation or judging). \subsection{From measurement to solution design: what actually ships}\label{sec:design}
The routing map answers a \textbf{measurement} question: which engine is best on each field, and by how much. A deployment answers a different question: \textbf{which of those wins are worth operating.} Measurement should be exhaustive; design should be ruthless. The design rule is the product of three quantities already measured: route a field only where (accuracy gain from routing) $\times$ (cost of that field being wrong, \S\ref{sec:task}) exceeds the standing cost of one more engine to version, monitor, and re-evaluate. Applied to the map:

\begin{table}[h]\centering\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}p{2.6cm}p{4.6cm}p{1.4cm}p{4.0cm}@{}}
\toprule
Field & Routing gain & Fail cost & Ship a dedicated engine? \\
\midrule
summary---long/rich & large; the core product & high & \textbf{Yes}---tuned student \\
summary---short/thin & $+29$ faithful, but only via the teacher & high & \textbf{Priced option}---only if the ${\sim}82$-min batch is acceptable \\
tone & $+50$ points (79.1 vs 29.0) & low & \textbf{Yes}---gain too large to ignore; one extra batch pass \\
depth & $+6$ over majority class & low & No \\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

The shipped configuration is therefore \textbf{two engines and one rule}---the tuned student everywhere, a single few-shot pass for \emph{tone}, with the teacher fallback for thin-source summaries held as a deliberate, latency-priced option---not the full map. That captures most of the measured routing benefit at a fraction of the operating surface. (Implementation note: routing runs \emph{batch-by-engine}---every article through the tuned student, then one few-shot pass for the routed field, merge---never per-article model swapping, which would thrash model loading.)

Two costs of routing this harness does not measure, stated plainly: \textbf{(1) cross-field coherence}---when \emph{tone} comes from one engine and the summary from another, nothing enforces agreement; an alarmed summary under an ``optimistic'' badge is a failure mode that exists \emph{only because of routing} (\S\ref{sec:future}); \textbf{(2) maintenance surface}---every routed engine is one more artifact to re-evaluate whenever the base model, teacher, or feed mix changes. The offline harness amortizes this here (one command re-scores every arm), but a team without one inherits the liability silently.

The distinction generalizes: the \emph{scientific} value of a per-field evaluation is the complete map---every winner at every granularity, including wins too small to use. The \emph{application} value is the smallest routed subset that pays. The map is the menu; the design is the order.

\section{Methods contribution}\label{sec:methods}
Beyond the routing table, the study contributes a \textbf{decomposed, reference-free, fully-local evaluation harness} for structured-output distillation. Each ingredient is individually established in the LLM-judge literature (\S\ref{sec:related}); the contribution is assembling them into a discipline that catches the ways a less controlled evaluation would overstate the method:
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{Per-sub-task decomposition} that localizes what distillation moves---without it, the faithfulness regression, the urgency overshoot, and the tone-labeling failure all vanish into one flattering average.
\item \textbf{Gap-closure-to-teacher} as the reporting scale, matched to the engineering goal, with absolute-delta and small-denominator caveats (\S\ref{sec:gapclosure}).
\item \textbf{Two non-distillation controls} (prompting, formatting) so ``distillation'' is credited only for what neither cheaper lever provides.
\item \textbf{A demonstration that reference-free faithfulness judging must use the full source}---a lead-only pass produced a $-9$\,pt gap that full-context grading shrank to $-4$ and localized. The single most transferable warning in the paper.
\item \textbf{Human-free grader validation} (negative control) and direction-only multi-judge reporting, so the conclusions reproduce offline from the judge cache with no API keys and no annotator.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Limitations}\label{sec:limits}
\begin{itemize}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{The reasoning-teacher question is not answered.} I trained on the teacher's final answers only and did not include an equal-size \emph{non-reasoning} teacher, so I cannot say whether the teacher's reasoning nature contributed anything beyond being a capable 8B model. The title and framing are scoped to ``an 8B reasoning teacher,'' not to ``reasoning was necessary.'' This is the most important limitation and the most valuable open question (\S\ref{sec:future}).
\item \textbf{Silver, not gold labels.} Classification ``accuracy'' is against panel \emph{consensus}, not human truth; agreement (0.70--0.91) bounds trust but does not establish correctness. The per-class confusion analysis (\S\ref{sec:classification}) quantifies the \emph{urgency} case---most of the tuned margin is majority-class alignment---but the deeper limitation stands: the ``gold'' labels are judge priors, not ground truth.
\item \textbf{Two-judge panel; magnitudes uncertain.} Directions are robust under each judge; exact percentages are not judge-invariant (\S\ref{sec:robust}). A two-judge ``consensus'' has no true majority on the ${\sim}27\%$ of items where judges disagree (\S\ref{sec:judges}); those labels inherit the tiebreak judge's biases.
\item \textbf{Faithfulness is reference-free LLM-judged, not entailment-verified.} Full-text grading removed the truncation artifact, but the metric is still a judge's claim-support call, not a formal NLI check. The negative control validates the gross case, not subtle in-domain fabrication (\S\ref{sec:negcontrol}). The residual gap and its short-article concentration should be confirmed with a MiniCheck-class verifier (\S\ref{sec:future}).
\item \textbf{The short-article finding rests on a small subgroup} ($n=22$, \S\ref{sec:faithful}). Consistent across seeds and robust to the truncation control, but ``concentrated in short sources'' is as strong as the data support.
\item \textbf{Seed variance is first-order on subjective fields} (\S\ref{sec:seeds}); the per-article bootstrap CIs do not fully propagate it, and the platform arm is a single run without a seed-variance estimate of its own.
\item \textbf{Third-order distillation.} \texttt{deepseek-r1:8b} is itself a distilled model and Qwen3-0.6B is itself a distillation product; the teacher's own quality caps what the student can inherit.
\item \textbf{Single task family.} One reader's feeds; the routing table is a hypothesis for related use cases (\S\ref{sec:assignment}), not a measured result there. No generalization to unseen sources is claimed.
\end{itemize}

\section{Future work}\label{sec:future}
Ordered by expected value.
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep2pt
\item \textbf{Run the equal-size non-reasoning-teacher arm} (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct / Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct)---the one experiment that converts ``I distilled an 8B teacher'' into an answer to ``does a \emph{reasoning} teacher help when distilling for \emph{non-reasoning} tasks at sub-1B?'' Highest value by far.
\item \textbf{A human-gold anchor on ${\sim}50$ items} to convert directional magnitudes into settled numbers and calibrate the judges---the single change that would most raise the evidentiary standing of every quantitative claim here.
\item \textbf{Confirm the residual faithfulness gap with formal entailment} (a MiniCheck-class NLI verifier over the whole article) and inject-fabrication tests to validate the judge's sensitivity to subtle in-domain hallucination (\S\ref{sec:negcontrol}).
\item \textbf{Turn the truncation observation into a measured curve} (600 / 1200 / 2400 / full chars) so the manufactured-regression artifact (\S\ref{sec:robust}) becomes a reference others can cite.
\item \textbf{Fix the short-article fabrication mode}---faithfulness-filtered training data, a decoding-time guard, or routing short sources to the teacher (\S\ref{sec:assignment}) as an immediate mitigation.
\item \textbf{Recover tone labeling}---test whether a class-balanced training set closes the one field prompting wins decisively.
\item \textbf{Reasoning-trace transfer arm} (train on \texttt{<think>} $+$ answer) to test directly whether traces help or hurt the small student.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
I set out to quantify what distillation buys when a structured-output pipeline moves from an 8B teacher to a sub-1B on-device student, with a shipped reader's enrichment batch as the test bed. On a commodity MacBook, a distilled 600M Qwen3-0.6B student runs the 500-article enrichment in \textbf{${\sim}7$ minutes instead of 5.4 hours}, recovers \textbf{most of the teacher's summary quality (59\% of the gap)} and \textbf{half of the classification gap}, and beats both non-distillation controls on summary quality. The recovery is uneven in ways that matter: on classification the aggregate only ties few-shot prompting (the win is field-specific---\emph{urgency}, \emph{frame}), \emph{depth} is untrustworthy for every small-model arm, and on summaries the student transferred the teacher's style and specificity slightly faster than its faithfulness, leaving a small deficit confined to short-source articles.

The verdict is therefore \textbf{not} ``distillation works'' or ``distillation hallucinates'' but a two-layer answer. The \emph{measurement} layer is the per-field map (\S\ref{sec:assignment}, scored as a system in \S\ref{sec:routing}): every field has a best engine, and the winners differ. The \emph{design} layer (\S\ref{sec:design}) is deliberately smaller: ship the tuned student everywhere, one few-shot pass for \emph{tone}, and hold the teacher fallback for faithfulness-critical thin-source prose as a priced option. Measure every field; ship only the routing that pays. The same training data pushed through a managed platform (different teacher, synthetic expansion) tied the DIY recipe on summary quality while inverting its classification profile---so even the choice of training pipeline is a per-field decision.

Two lessons generalize. \textbf{Decompose before you conclude---and check your instrument:} scoring the three sub-tasks apart made every real finding visible that an aggregate would hide, and grading against the full source rather than a truncated lead turned a $-9$\,pt ``regression'' into a $-4$\,pt short-article effect---in reference-free evaluation the measurement setup can be a larger effect than the model. \textbf{Separate the lever from the goal:} speed came from model size, quality-at-that-size from distillation; naming which lever does what turned a vague ``is it worth it'' into an actionable routing table. And one question I leave open rather than claim: I distilled a \emph{reasoning} teacher, but did not prove its reasoning was the reason it worked. That is the next experiment.

\section{Summary checklist and judge prompts}\label{app:checklist}
The checklist (8 binary checks $+$ topics), graded by the panel against the full article, fixed after the pilot and before scoring:
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep1pt
\item \textbf{faithful}---every factual claim is supported by the article (no hallucination/contradiction).
\item \textbf{thesis}---captures the article's central thesis or main finding.
\item \textbf{takeaway}---includes a concrete, specific takeaway (not vague/generic).
\item \textbf{length}---3 to 4 sentences.
\item \textbf{opening}---does NOT begin with ``This article'' or ``The article''.
\item \textbf{teacher-lens}---explains a concept accessibly or builds intuition.
\item \textbf{tech-lens}---addresses the technical/engineering angle.
\item \textbf{tone}---direct $+$ contemplative $+$ optimistic (not alarmist or generic).
\item \textbf{topics\_cover} (reported separately)---the listed topics capture the article's main themes.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Checklist judge prompt} (batched across arms, blinded): the judge receives the full article, then all arms' summaries under shuffled anonymous labels, and returns strict JSON keyed by label with a boolean per check. System prompt: \emph{``You are a strict grader. Judge each labeled summary against the article only. Output strict JSON keyed by label.''}

\textbf{Classification prompt:} the judge receives the full article and assigns exactly one label per field from the closed vocabularies in \S\ref{sec:task}; the panel vote is the proxy-gold (tiebreak per \S\ref{sec:judges}).

\textbf{Negative control:} the same checklist prompt, but a summary is paired with a mismatched article; \emph{faithful} collapses to 0\% ($n=30$).

\section{Reproducibility}\label{app:repro}
All headline metrics recompute offline from the cached judge log, with no API keys. The judge cache holds both the truncated (1200-char) and the canonical full-text responses, keyed by prompt; the \texttt{ARTICLE\_CHARS} environment variable selects which.

\begin{verbatim}
# Full-text scorecard (canonical): structure, classification, checklist, neg. control
ARTICLE_CHARS=100000 JUDGES_EXCLUDE=gpt-oss-120b,llama-3.1-8b node server/eval/compile.mjs
# Paired-bootstrap significance on the summary checklist:
node server/eval/paired_bootstrap.mjs data/eval/scores_gemini_nemotron_n93_fullctx.json
# Per-judge robustness (each judge alone; confirms directions):
ARTICLE_CHARS=100000 JUDGES_ONLY=gemini node server/eval/compile.mjs
ARTICLE_CHARS=100000 JUDGES_ONLY=nvidia node server/eval/compile.mjs
\end{verbatim}

Key files: test set $+$ teacher gold \texttt{data/eval/test.jsonl}; split \texttt{data/eval/split.json} (401/93); per-arm outputs \texttt{data/eval/arm\_*.jsonl} (temperature 0, with measured \texttt{durationMs}); canonical full-text scorecard \texttt{data/eval/scores\_gemini\_nemotron\_n93\_fullctx.json}; analysis engine \texttt{server/eval/compile.mjs}; significance \texttt{server/eval/paired\_bootstrap.mjs}; judge panel \texttt{server/eval/judges.mjs}; design notes \texttt{paper/evaluation\_design.md}.
